About me.

I am a Master's student in Theoretical Physics at Sapienza University of Rome. My academic interests lie at the intersection of quantum information, quantum cryptography, and photonic quantum technologies. I am currently working on my Master's thesis, focusing on quantum cryptographic protocols and their implementation in photonic platforms.

Throughout my studies I have developed a strong interest in both the theoretical and experimental aspects of modern physics. I enjoy exploring how abstract concepts from quantum mechanics can be translated into practical technologies for communication, computation, and information processing.

Beyond physics, I enjoy learning foreign languages and practising sports. These activities help me maintain curiosity, discipline, and a balanced perspective, qualities that I also bring to my academic work and research.
